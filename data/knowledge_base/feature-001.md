# Feature Request Process and Product Roadmap

When customers request new features or improvements, the request should be logged and the customer informed about how the product roadmap works.

## Collecting a feature request

Capture the following from the customer ticket:
- What they want to do (the job-to-be-done, not just the feature name)
- Why the current product does not meet the need
- How often they encounter this limitation
- Any workaround they currently use

## Logging the request

All feature requests must be logged in the product feedback tracker. Search for duplicates before creating a new entry — if a duplicate exists, add the customer's upvote and note.

## What to tell the customer

- We log every feature request and the product team reviews them quarterly.
- We cannot commit to specific timelines.
- Popular requests are more likely to be prioritised.
- Subscribe to the public changelog for updates.

## What not to promise

- Do not promise a feature will be built.
- Do not share internal roadmap dates.
- Do not tell a customer their request is "planned" unless it is already public.

## Routing

Feature requests route to the product team. They do not require urgent handling unless combined with a critical bug workaround.
